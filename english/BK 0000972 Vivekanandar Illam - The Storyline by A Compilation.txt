
Vivekanandar Illam (earlier known as Ice House, Castle Kernan, Marine Mansion) is an important place for the Ramakrishna Movement in South India. Swami Vivekananda had stayed here for nine days. It was at the Illam that Swami Ramakrishnananda, a direct disciple of Bhagvan Sri Ramakrishna sowed the seeds of now well-known activities of Sri Ramakrishna Math at Chennai. Thus the history of the Ramakrishna Movement is invariably and deeply associated with the Vivekanandar Illam. In this book, the following pages contain the storyline of the Vivekananda Illam through 60 facts. It chronicles the history of the Ice House building and also eminent people who either lived here or visited it at different points of time.
